# Water

The water modelling component of NEST-Pakistan is planned for future development. This section is a placeholder.
:::

## Overview

Pakistan is one of the most water-stressed countries in the world. Its water system is dominated by the Indus basin, which feeds extensive canal irrigation and supplies drinking water to a large and rapidly urbanising population. Climate change is altering glacier melt patterns and monsoon dynamics in ways that have profound implications for water availability.

The planned water component of NEST-Pakistan will model:

- Surface water availability and allocation across the Indus basin
- Groundwater extraction for agriculture and domestic use
- Water requirements for energy production (hydropower, thermal cooling)
- Climate change impacts on water supply

## Linkages to Energy and Agriculture

Water is a critical input to both the energy system (hydropower generation, thermal plant cooling) and agriculture (irrigation). These linkages will be captured through shared resource constraints in the integrated nexus framework.
