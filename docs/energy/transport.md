# Transport

Transport is Pakistan's second-largest energy-consuming sector and a growing source of CO₂ and air pollutant emissions. It is dominated by road transport, particularly motorcycles and light-duty vehicles. Electrification of road transport is the primary mitigation lever in the NDC and Net Zero scenarios.

## Sector overview

Pakistan's transport energy demand is met almost entirely by oil products (petrol, diesel, CNG for road; jet fuel for aviation). The sector undergoes dramatic fuel switching under Net Zero, with electricity's share of transport energy rising from negligible levels in 2025 to 62–80% by 2050, and hydrogen supplying a further 8–21%. Together these shifts reduce transport CO₂ emissions by 86–88% relative to CM under NZ.

## Road transport

### Passenger vehicles

Pakistan has one of the highest motorcycle densities in Asia. The light-duty vehicle fleet is growing rapidly. Key near-term policy: the New Energy Vehicle Policy targets a 30% EV share of new vehicle sales by 2030, implemented in the model through enhanced BEV share weights and simultaneous suppression of ICE additions.

Under NDC-C, transport achieves the largest reductions of any demand sector — CO₂ emissions fall by 57–72% relative to CM. Under NZ, electricity dominates transport energy at 62–80% by 2050.

### Freight

Road freight is diesel-dominated. Heavy-duty trucks carry the majority of Pakistan's goods movement. In GCAM-Pakistan, trucks account for roughly 70% of transport liquids remaining by 2050 under NZ, reflecting their relative difficulty to electrify within the modelling period.

## Rail

Pakistan Railways operates a diesel network. Rail is represented in the model; electrification of main corridors is included as a potential option under higher-ambition scenarios.

**Data sources:** Pakistan Economic Survey 2024–25

## Aviation

Domestic and international aviation is represented as a demand-linked final energy node. Aviation is difficult to decarbonise; jet fuel remains the primary fuel across all scenarios within the modelling horizon.

**Data sources:** Pakistan Economic Survey 2024–25
