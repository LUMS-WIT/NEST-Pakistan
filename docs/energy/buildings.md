# Buildings

The buildings sector in Pakistan encompasses residential and commercial energy use for space conditioning, cooking, water heating, and lighting and appliances. It is the largest end-use sector by final energy consumption and is closely tied to urbanisation and income growth trajectories under SSP2.

## Sector overview

Buildings energy demand in Pakistan is characterised by:

- Heavy reliance on biomass (firewood, dung cake) in rural residential use
- Rapidly growing electricity demand for cooling in urban areas
- LPG and natural gas for cooking in urban households
- Kerosene for lighting in off-grid areas (declining with rural electrification)

| Sub-sector | Primary fuels | Key driver |
|---|---|---|
| Residential — rural | Biomass, LPG | Rural population, income |
| Residential — urban | Electricity, gas, LPG | Urban population, cooling degree days |
| Commercial | Electricity, gas | GDP, floor area |

### Urban residential

Urban energy demand is growing rapidly, driven by cooling load from rising temperatures and urbanisation. Space cooling (air conditioning) is the fastest-growing end use.
