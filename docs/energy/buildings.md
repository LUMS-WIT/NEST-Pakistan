# Buildings

The buildings sector in Pakistan encompasses residential and commercial energy use for space conditioning, cooking, water heating, lighting, and appliances. It is closely tied to urbanisation and income growth trajectories under SSP2.

## Sector overview

Buildings undergo substantial electrification under higher-ambition scenarios. Electricity's share of buildings final energy rises from 20–37% in 2025 to 52–75% by 2050 under Net Zero, while gas and liquid fuel consumption decline by 94–100% and 64–75% respectively. As a result, buildings CO₂ emissions fall by 90–94% relative to CM under NZ.

Under NDC-C, however, buildings emissions fall by only 11–31%, indicating that deeper decarbonisation of the sector requires the stronger price signals and stricter constraints associated with Net Zero.

In both models, buildings are the primary recipient of hydrogen under NZ (alongside industry and transport), used for heat applications that are difficult to directly electrify.

| Sub-sector | Primary fuels (base year) | Key driver |
|---|---|---|

| Residential — urban | Electricity, gas, LPG | Urban population, cooling degree days |

**Data sources:** Pakistan Economic Survey 2024–25; NEPRA State of Industry Report

## Residential

### Rural residential

Rural households represent the majority of Pakistan's population and rely heavily on traditional biomass for cooking and heating. Rural electrification and LPG penetration are ongoing transitions captured in the CM baseline.

### Urban residential

Urban energy demand is growing rapidly, driven by cooling load from rising temperatures and urbanisation. Space cooling (air conditioning) is the fastest-growing end use. The model captures this through cooling-degree-day driven useful energy service demands.

**Data sources:** Pakistan Economic Survey 2024–25

## Commercial

Commercial buildings (offices, retail, hotels, hospitals, schools) are significant electricity consumers. Demand is driven by GDP and urban floor area expansion under SSP2.

**Data sources:** NEPRA State of Industry Report; Pakistan Economic Survey 2024–25
