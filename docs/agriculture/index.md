# Agriculture

The agriculture modelling component of NEST-Pakistan is planned for future development. This section is a placeholder.
:::

## Overview

Agriculture accounts for approximately 19% of Pakistan's GDP and employs nearly 40% of the labour force. It is the largest consumer of water (over 90% of freshwater withdrawals) and a significant consumer of energy through irrigation pumping, food processing, and fertiliser production.

The planned agriculture component of NEST-Pakistan will model:

- Crop production and land use across Pakistan's agro-ecological zones
- Water demand for irrigation by crop and region
- Energy inputs to agriculture (mechanisation, fertiliser, processing)
- Land-use change and associated emissions
- Vulnerability and adaptation to climate impacts

## Linkages to Energy and Water

Agriculture is deeply coupled to the energy and water systems. Irrigation pumping is a major electricity load; food processing depends on reliable energy supply; and agricultural water demand drives the bulk of surface and groundwater extraction. These dependencies will be captured through shared constraints in the integrated nexus framework.
